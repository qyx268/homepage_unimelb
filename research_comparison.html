Dark-ages reionization and galaxy formation simulation VII:

 

in preparation
Comparison between hydrodynamic simulations and semi-analytic models
Yuxiang Qin1, Simon J. Mutch1, Alan R. Duffy2, Gregory B. Poole1, Paul W. Angel1, Paul M. Geil1, Andrei Mesinger3 and J. Stuart B. Wyithe1

1School of Physics, University of Melbourne, Parkville, VIC 3010, Australia
2Centre for Astrophysics and Supercomputing, Swinburne University of Technology, PO Box 218, Hawthorn, VIC 3122, Australia
3Scuola Normale Superiore, Piazza dei Cavalieri 7, I-56126 Pisa, Italy
abstract
As part of the Dark-ages Reionization And Galaxy formation Observables Numerical Simulation (or DRAGONS) project, we investigate the effect of different physical processes including reionization on galaxy formation using both hydrodynamic simulations (HYDROs) and semi-analytic models (SAMs). Comparing these two numerical methods provides an improved understanding of the essential physics of early galaxy formation, and also helps us comprehend and improve the formulated physics implemented in these models. In this work, we run a set of HYDROs and relevant SAMs, each of which contain similar recipes to model the critical physics including gas cooling, star formation, supernovae feedback, metal enrichment, merger and reionization. We match galaxies between HYDROs and SAMs. We then compare the properties and evolutions of matched galaxies at high redshift (z≥5). We find a good agreement between two models and observation, suggesting most of the principal physics are effective to simulate the galaxy formation at early times in both models. However, the scatter comes from the essential difference between two modelling techniques. For instance, in the current SAM, the mechanism of forming stars is too bursty and hence has difficulty in matching the corresponding HYDRO on the star formation rate history and stellar mass evolution. However, these can be modified by introducing a redshift dependent star formation efficiency. Moreover, in order to account for the baryonic effects on halo properties, modifications of halo merger trees are also necessary.

Key words: cosmology: reionization — galaxies: formation — method: hydrodynamic simulation — method: semi-analytic model
